About me

A fellow traveller in this wonderful, at times turbulent adventure called life

From early on I was drawn to the ways the outer world reflects our inner world, and to what makes us resilient, hopeful and beautifully human. With all our flaws, contradictions and strengths. My life has been shaped by a few core themes that deeply inform my work.

I Belonging to community and self

Raised in a Mediterranean culture that values connection, warmth and community. It took a lifetime of experimentation to find the balance between belonging and autonomy: where loyalty to our systems meets self-determination.

II Immigration and the search for home

Moving to the Netherlands brought forward profound questions about belonging. What does home mean when you no longer live in the landscape of your birth? How do you find an inner home when the outer world has shifted?

III Loss, grief and rebuilding trust

The early death of my husband exposed me to the rawness of loss and the slow, fragile process of returning to life. It taught me about the quiet strength that emerges when we honour what has been.

IV Motherhood and resilience

Being a mother has encouraged me to explore what helps us flourish, and how resilience grows when we accept our own makeup and the makeup of those we love.

V Working with Gen Z

Working daily with Gen Z has made me deeply curious about hope: what it means to walk into adulthood when the world feels unpredictable, and how young people find direction, courage and meaning.

Trained as a systemic coach at the Bert Hellinger Institute. As a native speaker of English, raised bilingually, I am at home in both languages and in the deep connection between language and embodied emotion. Sessions are available in English and Dutch.
